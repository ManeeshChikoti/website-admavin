Stories of emerging OOH Practices around the world

Maxamtech Digital Ventures and Lemma Team up to Bring 'Metaverse' Advertising to the Forefront of DOOH

Lemma, a leading supply-side platform for large format ads, has announced its collaboration with Maxamtech Digital Ventures to launch metaverse billboards in the real world for DOOH clients. Brands can now create a dual experience for spectators in both the virtual and actual worlds through Lemma, resulting in dual impact. The partnership between Lemma and Maxamtech will assist global brands in creating brand impressions on untapped audiences through virtual media in the Metaverse. Billboard advertising in the Metaverse is currently relatively affordable, making it a potentially lucrative opportunity to target new audiences entering the Metaverse.

Bata Pioneering 3D Campaign In India

Bata India came up with a 3D OOH billboard that utilizes an anamorphic image content creation format thereby making content appear in 3 dimensions, which are visually compelling. Bata has used this innovative OOH technology to highlight the variety of sneakers it offers with the Sneaker Studios launched across 300+ stores across the country.

From Kashmir TO Kerala - Emergence Of Floating OOH And audience

Times OOh Wins A Rights For GMR Goa International Airport, Mopa

Times Innovative Media Limited (Times OOH) has secured the advertising rights for Manohar International Airport, a greenfield airport at Mopa in North Goa, for a period of 20 years. Developed by GMR group, the Mopa airport is a full-service airport designed to cater to domestic and international passengers and freight services, with a target passenger capacity of 13 million travelers per annum.

Rev Up Your Brand's Exposure with the High-Speed Delhi-Mumbai Expressway OOH Route!

The Delhi-Mumbai expressway, hailed as India's monumental infrastructure project, has put the OOH sector into high gear. As India's longest expressway, spanning 1,386 kilometers, it has significantly reduced travel time between Mumbai and Delhi to only 12 hours, down from nearly 24 hours. The expressway's completion is anticipated by January 2024, with progress being made on multiple routes. Recently, PM Narendra Modi inaugurated the Sohna to Dausa stretch, which is expected to cut the distance between Gurgaon and Jaipur by two hours.
